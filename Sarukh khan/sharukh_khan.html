Biography

Shah Rukh Khan, born on November 2, 1965, in New Delhi, India, is an Indian actor, film producer, and television personality. Known as the "King of Bollywood," he has appeared in more than 80 Hindi films and earned numerous accolades, including 14 Filmfare Awards.

Career

Shah Rukh Khan started his acting career in the late 1980s with television series such as "Fauji" and "Circus." He made his Bollywood debut in 1992 with "Deewana" and quickly rose to fame with iconic roles in films like "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge," "Baazigar," "Dil Se," and "My Name Is Khan."

Personal Life

Shah Rukh Khan is married to Gauri Khan, and the couple has three children: Aryan, Suhana, and AbRam. Besides his acting career, Khan owns the Kolkata Knight Riders cricket team and is actively involved in various philanthropic activities.
